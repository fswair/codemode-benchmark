--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Simple flat list remains unchanged. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Single level of nesting is flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested list to a single level. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4]]], 5])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Empty list returns an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: Lists containing only empty lists result in an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Mixed types including strings within lists. ---
Code: flatten(["a", ["b", "c"], "d"])
Output: ['a', 'b', 'c', 'd']

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Handling of None, Booleans, and Floats within the list. ---
Code: flatten([1, None, [True, False], 3.14])
Output: [1, None, True, False, 3.14]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Extreme nesting of a single element. ---
Code: flatten([[[[1]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: List containing empty lists and multiple nesting levels. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], [], [4, [5]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Non-list iterables (like range) are treated as single elements, not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([range(3)])
Output: [range(0, 3)]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Idempotency check: flattening a flattened list returns the same result. ---
Code: result = flatten([1, [2, 3]]); flatten(result) == result
Output: True

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Tuples inside the list are treated as atoms and not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([(1, 2), [3, 4]])
Output: [(1, 2), 3, 4]

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Dictionaries inside the list are treated as atoms and not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([{"a": 1}, [{"b": 2}]])
Output: [{'a': 1}, {'b': 2}]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Different nesting structures containing same elements in same order flatten to same result. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2], 3]) == flatten([[1], 2, [3]])
Output: True

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Verify return type is always a list. ---
Code: isinstance(flatten([1, [2]]), list)
Output: True
