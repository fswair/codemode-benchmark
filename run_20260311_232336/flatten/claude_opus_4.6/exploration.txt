--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Happy path: simple nested list ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], [4, [5]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Already flat list ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3, 4, 5])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Empty list ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Deeply nested single element ---
Code: flatten([[[[[[42]]]]]])
Output: [42]

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: Nested empty lists ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[], []]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Mixed types as elements (int, str, float, bool, None) ---
Code: flatten([1, 'hello', [3.14, True, [None]]])
Output: [1, 'hello', 3.14, True, None]

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Single element list ---
Code: flatten([42])
Output: [42]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: List with only one nested empty list ---
Code: flatten([[]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Return type is list ---
Code: type(flatten([1, [2]])) is list
Output: True

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Flattening preserves order ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4], [5, 6]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Idempotency: flattening an already flat list returns same content ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3]) == flatten(flatten([1, 2, 3]))
Output: True

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Tuples inside list are kept as-is (not recursed into) ---
Code: flatten([(1, 2), [3, (4, 5)]])
Output: [(1, 2), 3, (4, 5)]

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Dicts inside list are kept as-is ---
Code: flatten([{'a': 1}, [{'b': 2}]])
Output: [{'a': 1}, {'b': 2}]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Large flat list ---
Code: flatten(list(range(1000))) == list(range(1000))
Output: True

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Two-level nesting with multiple sublists ---
Code: flatten([[1], [2], [3], [4], [5]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 16 [normal]: Mixed nesting depths ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4, [5]]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 17 [normal]: List containing strings (strings are not recursed into) ---
Code: flatten(['abc', ['def', ['ghi']]])
Output: ['abc', 'def', 'ghi']

--- Snippet 18 [normal]: Result length matches total non-list leaf count ---
Code: len(flatten([1, [2, 3], [[4, 5], 6]])) == 6
Output: True

--- Snippet 19 [normal]: Composition: flatten of concatenated lists equals concatenation of flattens ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3]]) + flatten([[4], [5, 6]]) == flatten([[1, 2], [3], [4], [5, 6]])
Output: True
