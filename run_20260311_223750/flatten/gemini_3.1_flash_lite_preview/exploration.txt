--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Flat empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Simple flat list of integers. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Single level of nesting. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], 4]),
Output: ([1, 2, 3, 4],)

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Multiple levels of nesting. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, 4], 5], 6])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: Deeply nested empty lists. ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: List containing heterogeneous types. ---
Code: flatten([1, 'a', 2.5, True])
Output: [1, 'a', 2.5, True]

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Asymmetric nesting. ---
Code: flatten([[1], 2, [3, [4]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Deeply nested single element. ---
Code: flatten([[[[[1]]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: List containing an empty sub-list. ---
Code: flatten([1, [], 2])
Output: [1, 2]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Nested lists of strings. ---
Code: flatten(['a', ['b', ['c']]])
Output: ['a', 'b', 'c']

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Identity property for flat structures. ---
Code: flatten([]) == [] and flatten([1, 2]) == [1, 2]
Output: True

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Verifying the length of the flattened result. ---
Code: len(flatten([[[1, 2], 3], 4]))
Output: 4

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Mixed nesting depths. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2], [[3]], [[[4]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Increasing depth of lists. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, [3, 4, [5, 6, [7, 8]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Alternating flat and nested segments. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2], 3, [4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

Code: flatten(None)

Code: flatten("not a list")
RAISED TypeError: Expected list, got str
