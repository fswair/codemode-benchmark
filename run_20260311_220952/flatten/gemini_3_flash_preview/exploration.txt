--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Simple flat list remains unchanged. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: One level of nesting is flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested list is fully flattened to one level. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Empty list returns an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: List containing only empty lists of various depths results in an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Lists containing strings are flattened correctly. ---
Code: flatten(["a", ["b", "c"], [["d"]]])
Output: ['a', 'b', 'c', 'd']

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: List with mixed types (None, bool, float) is flattened. ---
Code: flatten([1, None, [True, False], 3.14])
Output: [1, None, True, False, 3.14]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Deeply wrapped single element is extracted. ---
Code: flatten([[[1]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Mixed structure of atoms and lists. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], 4, [5, [6]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Tuples inside the list are treated as atoms and not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2], (3, 4), [5]])
Output: [1, 2, (3, 4), 5]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Dictionaries inside the list are treated as atoms. ---
Code: flatten([{"key": "val"}, [7, 8]])
Output: [{'key': 'val'}, 7, 8]

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Complex symmetric nesting. ---
Code: flatten([[[1, 2], 3], [4, [5, 6]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Idempotency: Flattening an already flattened list results in the same list. ---
Code: flatten(flatten([[1, [2]], [3]]))
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Verify the length of the resulting flattened list. ---
Code: len(flatten([1, [2, 3], [4, [5, 6]]]))
Output: 6

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Other iterables like range are treated as atoms. ---
Code: flatten([range(3), [4, 5]])
Output: [range(0, 3), 4, 5]
