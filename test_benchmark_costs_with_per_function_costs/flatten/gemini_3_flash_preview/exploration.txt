--- Snippet 1 [normal]: A simple flat list should return an identical list. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: A list with one level of nesting is flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested lists are fully flattened to a single level. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: An empty list should return an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: A list containing only empty lists should return an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Strings are treated as atoms and not further flattened/split. ---
Code: flatten(["a", ["b", ["c"]]])
Output: ['a', 'b', 'c']

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Heterogeneous lists containing different types (int, str, float, bool, None). ---
Code: flatten([1, "mixed", [2.5, True], None])
Output: [1, 'mixed', 2.5, True, None]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Duplicate values and duplicate nested structures are preserved. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], 1, [2, 3]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Flattening lists generated by range conversion. ---
Code: flatten([list(range(3)), [list(range(3, 6))]])
Output: [0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Idempotency: flattening an already flattened list results in the same list. ---
Code: flatten(flatten([[1, [2]]]))
Output: [1, 2]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Single element nested deep within multiple layers. ---
Code: flatten([[[[1]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Empty lists mixed with elements are removed during flattening. ---
Code: flatten([1, [], 2])
Output: [1, 2]

--- Snippet 21 [normal]: Handling of special float values like NaN and Infinity as atoms. ---
Code: flatten([float('nan'), float('inf'), -float('inf')])

--- Snippet 22 [normal]: Tuples containing integers are treated as atoms and not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[(1, 2), (3, 4)], [(5, 6)]])
Output: [(1, 2), (3, 4), (5, 6)]

--- Snippet 23 [normal]: Dictionaries containing lists are treated as atoms; internal lists are not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[{"key": [1, 2]}], {"other": "value"}])
Output: [{'key': [1, 2]}, {'other': 'value'}]

--- Snippet 24 [normal]: Interleaved empty lists and populated lists at the same nesting level. ---
Code: flatten([[], [1], [], [2, 3], []])
