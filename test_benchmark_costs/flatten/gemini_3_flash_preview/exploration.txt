--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Basic flat list remains unchanged. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Single level of nesting is flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested list is flattened to a single level. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4]]], 5])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Empty list returns an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: List containing only nested empty lists results in an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: List with various non-list types at the top level. ---
Code: flatten([1, "string", None, True])
Output: [1, 'string', None, True]

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Mixed types within nested structures are preserved. ---
Code: flatten([1, ["a", [None]], [[True]]])
Output: [1, 'a', None, True]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Extreme nesting of a single element. ---
Code: flatten([[[[1]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Composition with list concatenation. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3] + [[4, 5]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Idempotency: flattening an already flattened list results in the same list. ---
Code: flatten(flatten([[1, [2]], [3]]))
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Verify return type is a list. ---
Code: result = flatten([1, [2, 3]]); isinstance(result, list)
Output: True

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Structural independence: different nesting of same elements yields same flat result. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3]]) == flatten([[1, 2], 3])
Output: True

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Non-list iterables (like range) are treated as atoms, not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([range(3), [4, 5]])
Output: ['range(0, 3)', 4, 5]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Dictionaries inside lists are treated as atoms. ---
Code: flatten([{ "a": 1 }, [ { "b": 2 } ]])
Output: [{'a': 1}, {'b': 2}]

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Complex mix of shallow and deep nesting. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], [4, [5, 6]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]
