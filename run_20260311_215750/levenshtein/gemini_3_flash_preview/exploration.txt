--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Typical happy path (famous example) ---
Code: levenshtein("kitten", "sitting")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Two empty strings (zero distance) ---
Code: levenshtein("", "")
Output: 0

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Two identical strings (zero distance) ---
Code: levenshtein("abc", "abc")
Output: 0

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: One empty string (insertion only) ---
Code: levenshtein("", "abc")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: One empty string (deletion only) ---
Code: levenshtein("abc", "")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Completely different strings of same length (substitution) ---
Code: levenshtein("abc", "def")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Single char vs multiple (multiple insertions) ---
Code: levenshtein("a", "abc")
Output: 2

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Multiple vs single char (multiple deletions) ---
Code: levenshtein("abc", "a")
Output: 2

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Longer identical strings ---
Code: levenshtein("levenshtein", "levenshtein")
Output: 0

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Case sensitivity check (should count as edits) ---
Code: levenshtein("FLOMAX", "Volmax")
Output: 6

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Numeric strings (valid as strings) ---
Code: levenshtein("123", "123456")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Whitespace strings (different counts) ---
Code: levenshtein(" ", "  ")
Output: 1

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Symmetry property: d(a, b) == d(b, a) ---
Code: levenshtein("kitten", "sitting") == levenshtein("sitting", "kitten")
Output: True

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Mix of substitution and identity ---
Code: levenshtein("book", "back")
Output: 2

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Suffix removal ---
Code: levenshtein("testing", "test")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 16 [normal]: Suffix addition ---
Code: levenshtein("test", "testing")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 17 [normal]: Classic complex edit case (3 edits) ---
Code: levenshtein("Saturday", "Sunday")
Output: 3

--- Snippet 18 [error]: First argument is None (wrong type) ---
Code: levenshtein(None, "hello")

Code: levenshtein("hello", None)

Code: levenshtein(123, "123")

Code: levenshtein(["a", "b"], "ab")
RAISED TypeError: Both arguments must be strings

Code: levenshtein("test", {"a": 1})
