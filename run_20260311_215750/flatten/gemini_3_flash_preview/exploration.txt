--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Simple flat list remains unchanged. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: List of lists is flattened one level. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested lists are fully flattened. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4]]], 5])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Empty list returns an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: List containing an empty list returns an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([[]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Multiple layers of empty lists return an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([[[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: List of strings behaves like numbers. ---
Code: flatten(["a", ["b", "c"], "d"])
Output: ['a', 'b', 'c', 'd']

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Mixed types including None and Bool inside the list are preserved. ---
Code: flatten([1, [None, 3], False])
Output: [1, None, 3, False]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Tuples inside the list are treated as non-list elements and not flattened. ---
Code: flatten([1, (2, 3), 4])
Output: [1, (2, 3), 4]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Dictionaries inside the list are preserved as-is. ---
Code: flatten([{"a": 1}, [{"b": 2}]])
Output: [{'a': 1}, {'b': 2}]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Duplicates are preserved in the flattened result. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 2], 1])
Output: [1, 2, 2, 1]

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: List of identical nested lists. ---
Code: flatten([[[1]] * 3])
Output: [1, 1, 1]

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Idempotency: flattening an already flattened list results in the same list. ---
Code: flatten(flatten([[1, [2]], [3]]))
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Verify return type is always a list. ---
Code: isinstance(flatten([1, [2]]), list)
Output: True

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Other iterables like range are treated as atoms if they aren't explicitly instances of list. ---
Code: flatten([range(3)])
Output: [range(0, 3)]

--- Snippet 16 [error]: Passing None triggers TypeError guard clause. ---
