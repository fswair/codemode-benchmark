--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Simple flat list remains unchanged. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Empty list returns an empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Singular level of nesting. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Deeply nested list exploration. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4, 5]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: Deeply nested empty lists. ---
Code: flatten([[[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: List with multiple empty sub-lists. ---
Code: flatten([1, [], [2, [], 3]])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: List containing various non-list types. ---
Code: flatten([None, False, 0, "string"])
Output: [None, False, 0, 'string']

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: List containing other iterables (tuples, sets, dicts) which should NOT be flattened. ---
Code: flatten([(1, 2), {3, 4}, {"a": 1}])
Output: [(1, 2), {3, 4}, {'a': 1}]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Single element nested deep. ---
Code: flatten([[[[[1]]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Mixed flat and nested elements. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], 4, [5, [6, 7]], 8])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Idempotency: flattening a flattened list results in the same list. ---
Code: flatten(flatten([[1], [2]])) == flatten([[1, 2]])
Output: True

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Check the length of the resulting flattened list. ---
Code: len(flatten([1, [2, 3], [4, [5, 6]]]))
Output: 6

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Flattening lists with floats. ---
Code: flatten([[1.1, 2.2], 3.3])
Output: [1.1, 2.2, 3.3]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Flattening lists generated by range/constructors. ---
Code: flatten([list(range(3)), list(range(3, 6))])
Output: [0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Flattening lists containing booleans. ---
Code: flatten([True, [False, True]])
