--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Simple flat list remains unchanged. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: One level of nesting is flattened. ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested list to a single level. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, [4, 5]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Empty list returns empty list. ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: Nesting consisting only of empty lists. ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Mixed types (non-lists) are preserved. ---
Code: flatten([1, "a", 2.5, True])
Output: [1, 'a', 2.5, True]

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: List containing None is flattened correctly. ---
Code: flatten([1, [None], 2])
Output: [1, None, 2]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Tuples are treated as leaf elements (not flattened). ---
Code: flatten([(1, 2), [3, 4]])
Output: [(1, 2), 3, 4]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Single element in deep nesting. ---
Code: flatten([[[[1]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: Irregularly shaped nested list. ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3], 4, [5, [6, 7]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Check equivalence of differently nested structures. ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3]) == flatten([1, [2], [[3]]])
Output: True

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Verify return type is always a list. ---
Code: isinstance(flatten([1, [2]]), list)
Output: True

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Idempotency: flattening an already flat list. ---
Code: flatten(flatten([[1, [2]], [3]]))
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Range objects are treated as leaf elements (not flattened). ---
Code: flatten([range(3), [4, 5]])

Code: flatten([True, [False, [True]]])
