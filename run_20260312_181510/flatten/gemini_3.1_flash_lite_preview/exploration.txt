--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Empty list input ---
Code: flatten([])
Output: []

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Flat list of integers ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Deeply nested list of integers ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, [3, 4], 5], 6])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6]

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Nested empty lists ---
Code: flatten([[], [[]], [[[]]]])
Output: []

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: List with mixed types (integers, strings, floats, booleans) ---
Code: flatten([1, "a", 2.5, True])
Output: [1, 'a', 2.5, True]

--- Snippet 6 [normal]: Mixed nested and non-nested elements ---
Code: flatten([1, [2], 3])
Output: [1, 2, 3]

--- Snippet 7 [normal]: Minimal nesting depth 1 ---
Code: flatten([[1]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 8 [normal]: Maximal nesting (depth 4) ---
Code: flatten([[[[1]]]])
Output: [1]

--- Snippet 9 [normal]: Non-homogeneous nesting structure ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, [3, 4], 5, [6, [7, 8]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8]

--- Snippet 10 [normal]: List containing None elements ---
Code: flatten([None, [1, None], [None]])
Output: [None, 1, None, None]

--- Snippet 11 [normal]: Uniform nested structure ---
Code: flatten([[1, 2], [3, 4]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4]

--- Snippet 12 [normal]: Right-skewed nested list ---
Code: flatten([1, [2, 3, [4, 5, [6, 7]]]])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7]

--- Snippet 13 [normal]: Left-skewed nested list ---
Code: flatten([[[[1, 2], 3], 4], 5])
Output: [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]

--- Snippet 14 [normal]: Idempotency check (flattening twice yields the same result) ---
Code: original = [1, [2, 3], 4]; flatten(original) == flatten(original)
Output: True

--- Snippet 15 [normal]: Consistency on simple flat lists ---
Code: flatten([1, 2, 3]) == flatten([1, 2, 3])
Output: True
