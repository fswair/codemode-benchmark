--- Snippet 1 [normal]: Empty list input ---
Code: group_by([], "id")
Output: {}

--- Snippet 2 [normal]: Standard grouping by a key present in all dicts ---
Code: group_by([{"name": "a", "type": "fruit"}, {"name": "b", "type": "veg"}], "type")
Output: {'fruit': [{'name': 'a', 'type': 'fruit'}], 'veg': [{'name': 'b', 'type': 'veg'}]}

--- Snippet 3 [normal]: Dict missing key maps to None key ---
Code: group_by([{"a": 1}], "missing_key")

--- Snippet 4 [normal]: Grouping when all values are unique ---
Code: group_by([{"a": 1}, {"a": 2}], "a")
Output: {1: [{'a': 1}], 2: [{'a': 2}]}

--- Snippet 5 [normal]: Grouping when all values are same ---
Code: group_by([{"a": 1}, {"a": 1}], "a")
